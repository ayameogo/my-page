The Story

I started as an economist — trained in forensic economic analysis, policy research at the French Mission to the United Nations, and financial modeling. That foundation shapes how I think about design: every decision has a cost, an opportunity, and a measurable outcome.

The shift to design wasn't a departure from that rigor — it was an expansion of it. I found that the best products aren't just well-designed; they're well-reasoned. They reflect clear thinking about what users need, what the business requires, and what the technology can actually deliver. I've spent 20+ years operating at that intersection.

I've led through two major enterprise transformations: building LegalZoom's design organization through IPO readiness and a $2B valuation, and leading ServiceTitan's 70+ person global design org through public-company operating cadence while simultaneously architecting the company's AI interaction layer from the ground up.

What I've learned: the hardest design work isn't the interface. It's the operating model. The org structure. The framework that lets a product stay coherent across seven teams, three time zones, and an AI layer that's rewriting what software can do. That's where I spend my time.

I'm French-born, raised across cultures, and have led global teams throughout my career. That perspective — understanding that systems need to work for people, not just personas — runs through everything I build.

"Design is shifting from artifact production to system orchestration. From UI ownership to product lifecycle ownership. From human-only to human + AI collaboration. That frontier is where I operate."
